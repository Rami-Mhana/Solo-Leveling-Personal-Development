Understanding Semantic Elements

<header>

Represents introductory content, typically a group of introductory or navigational aids. It may contain heading elements, logos, search forms, etc.

Use case: Page header, article header, section header

<nav>

Represents a section of a page that contains navigation links, either within the current document or to other documents.

Use case: Main navigation menu, table of contents, pagination

<main>

Represents the dominant content of the body of a document. There should be only one <main> element per page.

Use case: Central content of a web page, excluding headers, footers, and sidebars

<section>

Represents a standalone section of content that doesn't have a more specific semantic element to represent it.

Use case: Chapters, tabbed content, grouped thematic content

<article>

Represents a self-contained composition that is independently distributable or reusable, e.g., in syndication.

Use case: Blog posts, news articles, forum posts, product cards

<footer>

Represents a footer for its nearest sectioning content or sectioning root element.

Use case: Page footer, article footer, contact information, copyright data
